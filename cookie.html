Cookie Policy

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. These files store information about your actions and preferences (like login details, language, and display settings), so you don’t need to re-enter them each time you visit or navigate the site. They can also be used to track browsing habits and deliver personalized content, including targeted advertising.

How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to enhance your experience on our site. Disabling cookies may impact site functionality and certain features, so we recommend keeping them enabled unless you’re sure you don’t need them. Below, we detail how and why we use various cookies.

Types of Cookies We Use

Our website uses several types of cookies, each serving a different purpose:

1. Essential Cookies: These are vital for the site to function and cannot be turned off. They include actions like saving your privacy preferences, logging you in, or processing forms. Without these, parts of the website may not work properly.

2. Analytical/Performance Cookies: These allow us to understand how visitors use our site, so we can measure performance and improve. They collect data anonymously and show us which pages are popular and how users navigate the site.

3. Functionality Cookies: These cookies enhance your experience by remembering choices you make, like your preferred language or location. They offer more personalized functionality, and the data collected is typically anonymous.

4. Targeting/Advertising Cookies: These cookies deliver ads that are relevant to you. They track your activity on the site to help tailor advertising and limit how often you see the same ad. They may be shared with third-party advertisers.

Third-Party Cookies

Some of our cookies are provided by trusted third-party services, such as Google Analytics, which helps us understand user behavior and improve our content. These third-party cookies may track metrics like the duration of your visit and pages you view.

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You can choose to accept or reject cookies. Most browsers accept cookies by default, but you can usually modify your settings to decline them. Keep in mind, disabling cookies may affect the website’s performance and limit access to certain features.

Managing Cookies on Different Browsers

If you prefer to manage or disable cookies, you can do so via your browser’s settings. Here’s how to manage cookies on some of the most popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Go to "Settings," select "Privacy and Security," then manage cookies under "Cookies and other site data."
  • Mozilla Firefox: In "Options," head to "Privacy & Security" and adjust cookie settings under "Cookies and Site Data."
  • Microsoft Edge: Access "Settings," choose "Privacy, search, and services," and manage cookies under "Cookies and site permissions."
  • Safari: In "Preferences," click on "Privacy" to manage cookies.

Be aware that disabling cookies may cause some features of the website to malfunction.
